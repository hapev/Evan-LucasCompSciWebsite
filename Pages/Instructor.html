DR. JOHN LEWIS

About the teacher: The great Dr. John Lewis is currently a teacher at the Bishops’ School in San Diego, California. He has more than 20 years of experience architecting, developing, managing and supporting enterprise security software for companies like CA Technologies Inc. and Physicians Online. He has eight years of experience teaching, developing and coordinating software engineering diploma, bachelor’s and master’s courses at Napier University, Edinburgh. John earned his bachelor of science from Exeter University in England, and he holds a Ph.D. in artificial intelligence from Edinburgh University in Scotland. He is also the proud father of two Bishop’s alumni, Catriona '13 and Emma '17.

Q&A

  • Q: What was your first impression of the Bishop’s Community? A: A sense of belonging – back in 2007 when our daughter, Catriona, started Middle School. Little did I know that eleven years later I would truly be part of this wonderful community.
  • Q: What do you love most about teaching? A: Finding a way of explaining that makes sense – every student is unique.
  • Q: What is your greatest accomplishment? A: Cycling the “Million Dollar Challenge” last year with the Challenged Athletes Foundation (CAF) from San Francisco to San Diego in seven days. Life changing!
  • Q: Who is your hero/mentor? A: My brother Robert is my mentor – though he doesn’t know it – he lives in New Zealand and blazes the trail for me.
  • Q: What’s on your bucket list? A: I don’t believe in them. The best things happen unexpectedly.
  • Q: If you could travel in time, what time period would you visit and why? A: I wouldn’t – other times are overrated.
  • Q: If you had to sing karaoke, which song would you choose? A: Some Hawaiian song from a CD we bought in Maui and played endlessly on car trips there.
  • Q: What’s your biggest pet peeve? A: Faces “glued” to mobile phones.
  • Q: What’s the last book you read? A: Books are hard work for me. I love the short stories in the New Yorker.
  • Q: Who was your favorite teacher in school and why? A: Mr. Keeping, my sixth-grade math teacher. I still remember the parrot polygon joke!
  • Q: If you could have a super power, what would it be? A: To immobilize mobile phones
